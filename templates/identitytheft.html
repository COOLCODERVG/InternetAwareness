What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft occurs when someone steals your personal information and uses it to take your money, open credit accounts, file tax returns, make health insurance claims and more without your consent. Fortunately, there are many things you can do to protect yourself.

Signs Of Identity Theft

Unrecognized Bank or Credit Card Transactions.

Unexpected Bills or Statements

Calls From Debt Collectors

Surprise Credit Score Drops

Denial of Loan or Credit Applications

Unusual Activity on Your Social Security Account



Legal Penalties

A misdemeanor identity theft conviction can result in up to one year in the county jail, a fine of up to $1,000, and summary probation. A felony identity theft conviction can result in up to three years in jail, a fine of up to $10,000, and formal probation.
