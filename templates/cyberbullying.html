What is Cyberbullying?

Cyber Bullying is a kind of bullying or harassment that is done online. This is done by actual people. The intention like always is to harm the person on the other side except this time through a screen.

Signs Of Cyberbullying

Becomes upset, sad or angry during or after being online or using their phone.

Refuses to go to school or expresses anger or dissatisfaction with a specific class or school in general.

Expresses reluctance or refuses to participate in activities previously enjoyed.

Has an unexplained decline in grades.

Increasingly reports symptoms of illness for which he or she wants to stay at home.

Withdraws from family or friends without any proper reason.



Legal Penalties

While there are no exact laws about facing legal charges for verbal or online abuse there are rights that are protected by the US Department of Education and Department of Justice. Cyber Bullying does have its own consequences. Although there are no strict laws pointing towards Cyber Bullying or verbal abuse online The School or District that you are going to however do have every right to give you the necessary punishment. Depending on the offense the school or district can also expel or suspend a student.
